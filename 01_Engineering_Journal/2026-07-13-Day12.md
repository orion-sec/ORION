# ORION Engineering Journal — Day 12

**Date:** 13 July 2026  
**Project:** ORION Security Automation Platform  
**Focus:** Threat Intelligence Correlation and Risk Scoring Integration

---

## Objective

The objective of Day 12 was to introduce threat intelligence correlation into the ORION investigation pipeline.

Previously, ORION evaluated threat intelligence results individually. The goal was to begin building a correlation layer capable of combining intelligence findings and producing a unified threat verdict.

---

## Engineering Work Completed

### 1. Threat Correlation Engine

Created a new module:

`11_Modules/threat_engine.py`

Implemented the function:

`correlate_threat_intelligence()`

The correlation engine evaluates threat intelligence results and returns:

- Verdict
- Confidence level
- Number of intelligence sources
- Correlation reason

Supported verdicts currently include:

- Malicious
- Suspicious
- Clean

This introduces a dedicated threat decision layer between threat intelligence enrichment and risk scoring.

---

### 2. Correlation Pipeline Integration

Updated:

`07_Scripts/ioc_extractor.py`

Investigation Text

---

### 3. Correlation-Aware Risk Scoring

Updated:

`11_Modules/score.py`

The `score_ip()` function now accepts the threat correlation result.

Risk scoring can now prioritise correlated intelligence verdicts before evaluating an individual threat intelligence result.

Current correlation scoring logic:

- Correlated Malicious verdict → High Risk / Score 95
- Correlated Suspicious verdict → High Risk / Score 80
- Individual Malicious reputation → High Risk / Score 90
- Individual Suspicious reputation → High Risk / Score 75

Existing IP classification safeguards remain active for:

- Loopback addresses
- Private IP addresses
- Reserved addresses
- Public global IP addresses

## Engineering Lessons Learned

Today demonstrated the difference between enrichment and correlation.

Threat intelligence enrichment asks:

> What does a threat intelligence source know about this IOC?

Threat intelligence correlation asks:

> What conclusion should ORION reach when multiple intelligence findings are evaluated together?

I also learned that successful integration requires regression testing against existing logic.

Adding a new threat engine should not break existing classification controls such as private IP or loopback detection.

The regression tests confirmed that the existing ORION safeguards remained functional.
