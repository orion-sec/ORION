# ORION Engineering Journal

## Day 19 – Identity Enrichment, Business Context & Operational Decision Engine

### Objective

Today's objective was originally to build the Decision Engine. During architectural review, it became clear that ORION did not yet possess enough organisational context to make high-quality operational decisions.

Instead of rushing into decision making, the architecture was expanded to include Identity Enrichment and Business Impact Assessment before introducing the first Operational Decision Engine.

This ensures future decisions are based on both security evidence and business context.

---

## Work Completed

### 1. Identity Enrichment Engine

Created:

11_Modules/identity_enrichment.py

Purpose:

Extend extracted identities with organisational attributes.

Current placeholder attributes include:

- Job Title
- Country
- Office
- Account Type
- VIP Status
- Criticality
- Device Count
- Identity Source

The module establishes ORION's long-term identity data model while remaining independent of Microsoft Graph or Active Directory.

### 4. Business Impact Engine

Created:

Purpose:

### 6. Operational Decision Engine (Rule-Based)

Created:

Purpose:

Produce explainable operational recommendations using:

- Business Impact

- Decision Inputs

- Recommended Actions

### 7. Operational Decision Reporting

Added a dedicated report section:

### 8. Architectural Improvement

Removed duplicated Business Impact information from the Enriched Identity section.

Business Impact is now calculated exclusively by the Business Impact Engine.

This reinforces:

Single Responsibility Principle

and ensures one authoritative source for Business Impact.

---

## Architectural Decisions

Today's biggest architectural decision was postponing the Evidence Reasoning Engine.

Instead of building advanced reasoning immediately, ORION first gained:

Identity

↓

Identity Enrichment

↓

Business Impact

↓

Operational Decision

This ensures future reasoning engines will receive richer organisational context.

## Lessons Learned

A security platform should not make operational decisions using only technical evidence.

Business context must be evaluated before determining response priority.

Separating Contextual Risk from Business Impact produces more accurate and explainable operational decisions.

This marks ORION's transition from an investigation tool toward an enterprise security decision-support platform.
