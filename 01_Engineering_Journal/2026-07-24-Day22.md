# ORION Engineering Journal – Day 22

## Objective

Continue migrating the legacy ORION investigation workflow into the new ORION V2 pipeline architecture.

---

## Work Completed

Today I migrated two additional investigation stages into the ORION V2 execution pipeline.

The new stages added were:

1. Identity Enrichment
2. Business Impact Assessment

These stages were implemented as independent pipeline modules rather than embedded inside the main investigation script.

---

## Identity Enrichment Stage

Created a dedicated pipeline stage responsible for enriching extracted identities with organisational context.

The stage now performs:

- Identity enrichment
- Placeholder organisational lookup
- User criticality determination
- Account type identification
- Device ownership metadata
- Identity source tracking

The enriched identity is stored inside the shared pipeline results dictionary for downstream processing.

---

## Business Impact Stage

Created a dedicated Business Impact stage.

The stage consumes the enriched identity produced by the previous stage and calculates:

- Business Impact Score
- Impact Level
- Confidence
- Escalation Recommendation
- Supporting Evidence

This stage demonstrates true pipeline dependency where one stage consumes the output produced by another.

---

## Pipeline Evolution

The ORION V2 execution pipeline now consists of five sequential investigation stages:

1. Initialize Investigation
2. IOC Extraction
3. Identity Extraction
4. Identity Enrichment
5. Business Impact Assessment

Each stage now executes independently through the ORION Pipeline Engine.

---

## Pipeline Observability Improvements

The execution engine was enhanced with additional operational visibility.

New capabilities include:

- Human-readable stage names
- Stage numbering (e.g. [3/5])
- Execution duration per stage
- Pipeline execution summary
- Successful stage count
- Failed stage count
- Overall execution status

This significantly improves troubleshooting and future performance optimisation.

---

## Engineering Lessons Learned

A pipeline should never contain business logic.

Each stage should perform a single responsibility while the pipeline itself manages execution, ordering and observability.

Separating orchestration from investigation logic produces a modular architecture that is easier to maintain, extend and debug.
